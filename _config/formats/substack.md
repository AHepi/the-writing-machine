# substack.md — the newsletter essay

*Starter format. A subscriber newsletter: a personal, voice-forward essay that
can breathe a little longer than a column and talks to a returning reader.*

## 1. What it is

A newsletter piece — essayistic, personal, written to people who chose to hear
from this writer. Voice carries as much as argument.

## 2. Length norm

1,000–1,800 words; flexible — the form tolerates range.

## 3. Structure

- **Open on voice** — a personal entry point, an anecdote or a take, that earns
  the click open. Thesis arrives early but warmly (seeds `D1`).
- **The body** — the argument or exploration, in sections with breathing room;
  subheads welcome.
- **The turn** — the counter or the complication, handled honestly (seeds `D2`).
- **The sign-off** — a closing that feels like a person, not a conclusion. May
  gesture forward to the next piece.

## 4. Register defaults

First person, conversational, direct address ("you"). Contractions, asides, the
occasional fragment for rhythm. Warmer and looser than an essay; still clear.

## 5. Visuals policy

`auto` — a chart or a pulled-out table is at home in a newsletter; an embedded
image often helps. Defers to the brief's dial.

## 6. Memes & heat

Memes permitted if the audience file also permits — newsletters carry them
naturally. Heat tracks `provocation`; the voice can be opinionated by default.

## 7. Done-when

It reads like a real person wrote it to a real reader, the argument is intact,
the turn is honest, and the sign-off lands. Inside the (flexible) band.
