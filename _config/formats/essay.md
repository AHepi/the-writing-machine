# essay.md — the general-purpose prose piece (default format)

*The default. When the brief names no format, the piece is an essay: a single
argument, developed in prose, that takes the reader somewhere.*

## 1. What it is

A piece of argued prose with one governing idea, developed from a hook to a
payoff. Not a report, not a listicle — a line of thought the reader follows to a
place they hadn't been.

## 2. Length norm

1,400–1,700 words. (Used literally when `brief.length` is empty.)

## 3. Structure

- **Opening** — states the thesis plainly and early (seeds `D1`). A concrete
  hook, then the claim.
- **Development** — the argument's spine (`persuasion.md`): reason, evidence
  (F-tagged), built in coherent arcs.
- **The strongest counterargument** — steelmanned and answered (seeds `D2`).
- **Payoff** — what follows if the thesis holds. Closes on consequence, not on
  "in summary."

## 4. Register defaults

First person allowed but not required; address the reader directly. Formal
enough to be taken seriously, loose enough to read like a person thinking. Opens
on something concrete; never opens on definitions or throat-clearing.

## 5. Visuals policy

`auto` — visuals used where they genuinely beat prose (a trend, a comparison),
but the essay is a prose form first and does not reach for charts to look
rigorous. Defers to the brief's `visuals` dial.

## 6. Memes & heat

Heat tracks the `provocation` dial. Memes off by default; on only if both this
format note and the audience file permit (they mostly don't, for an essay).

## 7. Done-when

The reader can state the thesis and the one best reason for it, and feels the
counterargument was met rather than dodged. Length inside the band. Every D-item
present.
