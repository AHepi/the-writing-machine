# essay.md — format: essay (the default)

## 1. What it is / when to use
A general-purpose prose piece that develops a single idea with room to breathe.
The default when the brief doesn't ask for anything more specific. It argues or
explores; it has a thesis it earns rather than asserts; it reads as one
continuous thought, not a list with paragraphs.

## 2. Required parts  (→ deliverables)
- States or stakes its central idea early — the reader knows what's at issue
  within the first few paragraphs.
- Develops through a genuine arc; each section advances the idea, none merely
  restates it.
- Engages the strongest objection to its own claim before the end.
- Closes by landing the idea somewhere the opening only pointed at — an earned
  arrival, never a recap.

## 3. Length norm
1,200–1,800 words. (Overridable by the brief's `length`.)

## 4. Visuals policy
auto — admitted but not pushed. The essay is a prose form; a visual appears only
when prose genuinely can't carry the shape of the thing (a real dataset, a
structure). Default lean: prose.

## 5. Conventions & register
Continuous prose; paragraphs that flow, not bullet dumps. Headings optional and
sparing. Varied sentence length; a strong first line that isn't throat-clearing.
It ends because the idea is complete — never with "in summary" or "in
conclusion."

## 6. Imagery & meme licensing
One controlling metaphor, drawn from the audience's world, carried and *layered*
across the piece (per `imagery.md`). Supporting analogies stay in the same
source domain. Memes: default **no** for the essay, unless the audience strongly
licenses them and one earns its place argumentatively.
