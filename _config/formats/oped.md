# oped.md — the opinion column

*Starter format. A newspaper op-ed: short, sharp, one argument, a clear stake.*

## 1. What it is

A persuasive column with a single, timely claim and an explicit point of view.
Argues, doesn't survey.

## 2. Length norm

700–900 words. Tight by genre.

## 3. Structure

- **Lede** — a hook tied to the moment (a news peg, an event), thesis visible
  within the first two paragraphs (seeds `D1`).
- **The case** — two or three reasons, fast, each carried by one concrete
  example or figure (F-tagged).
- **The objection** — the strongest counter, named and answered in a tight beat
  (seeds `D2`).
- **The kicker** — a final line that lands the stake. Memorable, not a summary.

## 4. Register defaults

First person, direct, confident. Short paragraphs. Every sentence earns its
place — there is no room for throat-clearing in 800 words.

## 5. Visuals policy

`vetoed` by default — op-eds run as text. One small table is the most a column
ever carries, and only if the brief forces `visuals: on`.

## 6. Memes & heat

Heat naturally higher than an essay — an op-ed has a side. `provocation` raises
sharpness; the kicker can bite. Memes off (it's an op-ed, not a post).

## 7. Done-when

A reader knows exactly where the writer stands and why within the first 200
words, the objection was met, and the kicker lands. Inside the word band.
