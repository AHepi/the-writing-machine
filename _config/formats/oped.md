# oped.md — format: op-ed

*Draft — `[you]` file.*

## 1. What it is / when to use
A short, sharp argument for a general publication: one claim, urgently made, to
a reader who is deciding what to think about something live. Use when the goal
is to move opinion on a current question, fast, in limited space.

## 2. Required parts  (→ deliverables)
- A news peg or stakes in the first two sentences — why this, why now.
- One clear thesis, stated plainly and early.
- The single strongest counterargument named and answered.
- A close that demands something — a judgment, an action, a reframe — without
  a summary.

## 3. Length norm
650–900 words. (Hard ceiling; op-eds live or die on compression.)

## 4. Visuals policy
veto — text only. No charts or diagrams; the form is pure prose. A single
arresting statistic in-line is fine; a chart is not.

## 5. Conventions & register
Punchy, confident, public-voiced. Short paragraphs. A strong lede; a kicker
that lands. No hedging stacks ("it could be argued that perhaps"). Ends on the
kicker, never on "in conclusion."

## 6. Imagery & meme licensing
One sharp controlling image, audience-appropriate. Memes: **no** — the op-ed
register doesn't carry them regardless of audience.
