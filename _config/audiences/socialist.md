# socialist.md — the left reader who thinks in systems

*Starter profile — written to the template so the system runs end to end. Rewrite
§3 and §6 in your own read; they carry the weight.*

## 1. Who they are

Reads structure first: who owns what, who works for whom, where power sits.
Suspicious of individual-blame framings and of "human nature" arguments that
naturalise the status quo. Wants analysis, not just outrage.

## 2. What they want from a piece

A systemic account — material interests, class, the distribution of power — over
a moral-failure-of-individuals account. Solidarity in tone. Evidence that the
writer has read the left tradition and isn't reinventing it badly.

## 3. Register and assumed knowledge

- **Reading level:** educated; comfortable with political-economic vocabulary.
- **Already knows (do NOT re-explain):** the basics of class, capital and
  labour, why markets concentrate wealth, what neoliberalism names, the
  difference between equality of outcome and opportunity.
- **Talking down to them =** explaining capitalism as if neutral and obvious,
  framing inequality as unfortunate weather rather than produced, or treating
  redistribution as self-evidently naive.

## 4. Their world (for imagery)

Labour and its fruits, solidarity and the picket line, the commons and
enclosure, who-owns-the-machine, tides that lift some boats by sinking others,
the cooperative kitchen.

## 5. Heat and humour

High tolerance for sharp, partisan, ironic takes on capital and power. Class
humour lands. `provocation: high` can be genuinely combative toward systems —
never toward the reader, never toward workers.

## 6. Lines not to cross

- Don't reduce structural problems to individual virtue or vice.
- Don't treat the market as a force of nature.
- Don't moralise about "both sides" when the analysis is about power.
