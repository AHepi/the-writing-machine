# selection-method.md — how to choose and order facts

*Reference for stage 02. Stage 01 over-gathered; here you pay it back by cutting
to the facts that earn their place, and ordering them into an argument.*

---

## What survives the cut

A fact makes the factsheet only if it does at least one of these:

- **Serves a D-item** — it's load-bearing for something the brief requires.
- **Carries the argument's spine** — it's the reason, the evidence, or the
  steelman behind a claim (`persuasion.md`).
- **Earns a visual** — it's part of a trend, comparison, or structure a chart or
  table will show.

A fact that's merely *true and interesting* but serves none of the above is cut.
Interesting-but-irrelevant is the most common source of bloat and the first
thing to go.

## Setting the S-register

Translate stage 01's reliability note into a fixed register the draft must
respect:

- **S-strong** — primary/peer-reviewed/official. The draft may state it flatly.
- **S-medium** — reputable secondary. The draft states it, attributing where it
  matters.
- **S-weak** — single source, anecdote, interested party. The draft may only
  *raise* it ("some report…", "one account suggests…"), never assert it.

A claim written above its S-register is a stage 04 defect. When two sources
conflict, either resolve to the stronger or frame the disagreement openly —
never average them into a false certainty.

## Ordering into an argument

1. **Thesis-bearing facts first** — what the opening (D1) needs to state the
   claim concretely.
2. **The reason chain** — facts in the order the argument builds them, each beat
   picking up where the last left off (given-before-new, `pinker-prose.md`).
3. **The steelman's facts** — the best evidence for the *other* side, placed at
   the objection beat (D2). Don't hide it; it's what makes the reply credible.
4. **The payoff facts** — what follows if the thesis holds.

## Coverage check

Before writing the outline, confirm every D-item has a fact behind it and every
surviving fact has a beat to live in. A D-item with no fact is a flagged gap
(send it back toward stage 01). A fact with no beat is cut.
