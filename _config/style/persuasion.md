# persuasion.md — moves for pieces that argue

*Loads at stage 03 only when the brief's goal is to persuade or the format is
argumentative (op-ed, much of the essay). It sits on top of `pinker-prose.md`,
never against it: you persuade by letting the reader see, not by raising your
voice.*

## The frame: persuasion is showing, continued

Classic style already does the work. A reader who clearly sees what you see is
most of the way to agreeing. So the first persuasive move is always the prose
floor: clear window, concrete case, no fog. Rhetoric is what you add when the
view alone won't carry an unwilling reader across.

## The moves

- **Steelman first.** Put the strongest version of the opposing case before your
  answer to it. A reader who feels understood lowers their guard; a reader who
  feels strawmanned stops reading. (Every argumentative format makes this a
  deliverable.)
- **Concede what's true.** Give ground on the real points. Credibility is a
  budget; spend a little admitting the obvious and you can draw on it for the
  hard claim.
- **One thesis, load-bearing.** Persuasion diffuses across many claims. Pick the
  one the piece rises or falls on and make everything serve it.
- **Show the cost.** Honest argument names who pays and what the trade-off is.
  This is also a respect move — the audience profiles (conservative, socialist,
  trades) all reward writers who count costs and punish those who pretend
  there are none.
- **Earned emotion, placed late.** Feeling persuades, but only after reason has
  built the platform. Lead with the case; let the stakes land once the reader
  already sees them.

## The guards (so persuasion never becomes manipulation)

- No claim the factsheet can't source (`rules.md §provenance`). Persuasion runs
  on real facts or it's propaganda.
- No false suspense, no manufactured outrage, no implied consensus that isn't
  there.
- The condescension test still gates everything: a reader who notices they're
  being *worked* is a reader you've lost. Respect floor outranks any rhetorical
  gain.
- Provocation is dialed by the brief (`provocation: none/mild/sharp`), not by
  the writer's mood. Stay inside the dial.
