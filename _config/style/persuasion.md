# persuasion.md — how hard the argument leans

*Read at stage 02 (planning the argument's spine) and stage 03 (executing it).
This file sets the **mechanics of persuasion** that compose with the prose floor
(`pinker-prose.md`), the register (audience file), and the heat (`provocation`
dial). It never overrides the respect floor in `rules.md`.*

---

## The spine: claim → reason → evidence → objection → reply

Every persuasive piece carries this skeleton, however it's dressed:

1. **Claim** — the thesis, stated plainly and early. The opening section states
   it (this is usually `D1`). No burying the lede under context.
2. **Reason** — *why* the claim holds, in terms the audience already values.
   Borrow the audience's own commitments (audience file §3) as premises.
3. **Evidence** — F-tagged facts from the factsheet, at a register no stronger
   than their S-register allows.
4. **Strongest objection, steelmanned** — name the best counterargument, in a
   form its holders would accept (this is usually `D2`). A strawman is a defect.
5. **Reply** — answer the steelmanned objection, or concede the part that's
   right and show why the claim survives anyway.

## Leaning harder or softer — the `provocation` dial

- **`none`** — the default. Even-handed, lets evidence do the leaning. Concede
  freely. Aimed at a reader who could go either way.
- **`low`** — a clear point of view, openly held, but still fair to the other
  side. Sharper verbs, firmer conclusions.
- **`high`** — partisan heat. Rhetorical questions, irony, a named antagonist
  position. **The line that does not move:** heat raises *sharpness*, never
  lowers *respect for the reader* or *honesty about the evidence*. You may be
  unfair to an *argument*; you may not be unfair to a *fact* or condescending to
  the *reader*.

## Tools, and when each is licensed

- **Concession** — always available; it buys credibility cheaply. Concede the
  true part of the objection before answering it.
- **Reframing** — recast the question on terms favorable to the claim, but only
  if the reframe is *honest*, not a bait-and-switch.
- **Rhetorical questions, irony, repetition** — licensed at `provocation: low`
  and up, jointly with what the format and audience permit (an op-ed yes, a
  neutral explainer no).
- **Appeals to the audience's values** — always, and this is the engine: the
  most persuasive reason is one built from a premise the reader already holds.

## The honesty gate

Persuasion that misrepresents the evidence is not persuasion the workspace
ships. Steelman, don't strawman. Match register to S-register. Concede what's
true. A piece can lean as hard as the dial allows and still pass this gate —
that is the whole craft.
