# research-method.md — how to search, weigh, and tag

*Reference for stage 01. The method, not the contract — the contract
(`../CONTEXT.md`) decides what to produce; this decides how to do it well.*

---

## Search strategy

1. **Decompose by D-item.** The brief's deliverables tell you what the piece
   must do; each one names the evidence it needs. Search per D-item, not per
   vague topic.
2. **Start broad, then go primary.** A first pass establishes the landscape and
   the vocabulary; the second pass chases the primary sources — the study, the
   dataset, the original document — behind the secondary claims.
3. **Find the strongest counter-evidence on purpose.** D2 is usually "answer the
   strongest objection." You cannot answer what you didn't look for. Search
   *against* the goal as deliberately as you search for it.
4. **Stop when the picture stops changing.** When new searches return facts you
   already have, the evidence base is saturated for this piece's depth.

## Weighing sources — the S-register precursor

Record reliability as you go; stage 02 formalises it. A working scale:

- **Strong** — primary sources, peer-reviewed research, official datasets,
  first-hand documents.
- **Medium** — reputable secondary reporting, expert synthesis, textbooks.
- **Weak** — single anecdotes, unverified blogs, advocacy material, anything
  with an undisclosed stake.

A claim may be written in the draft only at a register its source supports
(`rules.md` §3). Note reliability now so stage 02 can enforce it later.

## Tagging

- One **F-tag** per discrete fact. If a "fact" bundles two claims, split it —
  visuals and the draft reference F-tags individually.
- Keep the claim atomic and quotable: `F7: median rent rose 12% in cities that
  capped it within five years [src: Diamond et al. 2019]`.
- Every F-tag points at a source ref that exists in `sources.md`.

## Honesty rules

- **Never invent data**, not even as a placeholder or an illustration. A fact
  that doesn't exist is a flagged gap, not a guess.
- **Mark contestation.** Where strong sources disagree, record both and tag the
  disagreement. The draft must handle it openly, not pick a side silently.
- **Disclose stakes.** If a source has an interest in the conclusion, note it —
  it changes the register the draft may use.
