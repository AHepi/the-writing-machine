# pinker-core.md — prose floor, distilled (≤350 tokens)

*The travel-size of `pinker-prose.md`. Inlined into tight prompts and into the
course package. When it and the long file disagree, the long file wins.*

**Prose is a window.** You have seen something real; angle clear glass so the
reader sees it too. The prose itself should disappear.

**Address an equal.** The reader is as smart as you and simply hasn't seen this
view yet. Not a student, not a complainant. This is why you never talk down.

**Show, don't announce.** No "in this section I will…", no "having shown X."
Just show the thing.

**Concrete over abstract.** Give the particular case the reader can picture; let
the abstraction be felt through it.

**Commit.** Cut hedging clutter ("it could perhaps be argued that in some
sense"). Caveats are content, not nervous tics.

**People do things.** Prefer active actors to nominalizations and default
passives. "The decision was made" hides who decided.

**Beat the curse of knowledge.** You forget what it's like not to know this
already. Name the thing before its abbreviation, show the step you'd skip, make
the implicit chain visible — without dumbing down.

**Lead the eye.** Given before new; the heavy phrase at the end; each sentence
connects to the last. Coherence the reader can feel, not signposting they must
endure.

**End because it's finished**, never with "in summary" or "in conclusion."
