# findings.md — constructor theory crash course

*Facts gathered against the goal and the D-list. Each carries an F-tag and a
source ref (resolved in `sources.md`). Reliability noted inline; stage 02 fixes
the formal S-register. Over-gathered slightly on purpose.*

## Origins & people (serves D2, D3, D8, D10)

- **F1**: Constructor theory was proposed by physicist David Deutsch; his
  foundational paper "Constructor Theory" appeared in the journal *Synthese* in
  December 2013. [S1, S2] — strong (peer-reviewed journal).
- **F2**: It has been developed since around 2012 by Deutsch together with
  Chiara Marletto at the University of Oxford. [S3, S6] — strong.
- **F3**: Chiara Marletto is a physicist at the University of Oxford (Wolfson
  College / Department of Physics) and wrote the popular book on the subject,
  *The Science of Can and Can't*, published 4 May 2021. [S4, S5] — strong.
- **F4**: David Deutsch is also known for foundational work in quantum
  computation — he defined the universal quantum computer in a 1985 Royal Society
  paper — and for two popular-science books, *The Fabric of Reality* (1997) and
  *The Beginning of Infinity* (2011). [S9, S10] — strong.
- **F5**: *The Fabric of Reality* argues that four "strands" of explanation —
  quantum physics, evolution, computation, and the theory of knowledge
  (epistemology) — are deeply interconnected. (Useful context for why Deutsch
  wants a framework that unifies information, life and physics.) [S10] — strong.

## The core reframing (serves D3, D4)

- **F6**: The "prevailing conception" of fundamental physics predicts what *will*
  happen from a system's initial conditions plus its laws of motion (dynamical
  equations). [S1, S3] — strong.
- **F7**: Constructor theory instead "expresses physical laws exclusively in
  terms of which physical transformations, or *tasks*, are possible versus which
  are impossible, and why." [S3] — strong (direct characterization).
- **F8**: Deutsch's stated motivation includes that some phenomena — notably the
  physics of information and the physics of life — are hard to capture in the
  language of trajectories and initial conditions. [S6] — strong (Marletto,
  Quanta interview).
- **F9**: A guiding heuristic of the programme, in Marletto's words: "Declaring
  something impossible leads to more things being possible." [S6] — medium
  (popular interview paraphrase of a real claim).

## Key definitions (serves D5)

- **F10**: A **task** is the abstract specification of a transformation as
  input–output pairs of attributes (states a substrate can have). [S3] — strong.
- **F11**: A **constructor** is an entity that can cause a task to occur *while
  retaining the ability to cause it again* — analogous to a catalyst, which
  drives a change while remaining able to do it again. [S3, S6] — strong.
- **F12**: A **substrate** is the physical system that a constructor acts on —
  the thing whose attributes get transformed. [S3] — strong.
- **F13**: A task is **impossible** if a law of physics forbids its being
  performed with arbitrarily high accuracy; otherwise it is **possible**. A
  "possible" task means a constructor for it can in principle exist, even if no
  one has built it yet. [S3] — strong.

## Counterfactuals (serves D6)

- **F14**: Constructor theory brings **counterfactual** statements — claims about
  what *could* or *could not* happen, not just what *does* happen — into
  fundamental physics. [S3] — strong.
- **F15**: This is why Marletto's book is titled *The Science of Can and Can't*:
  the programme treats statements of possibility/impossibility ("can"/"can't") as
  fundamental physical facts. [S4, S6] — strong.
- **F16**: Existing physics already contains hidden counterfactual/impossibility
  statements — e.g. energy conservation is equivalent to the statement that a
  perpetual-motion machine is impossible. [S6] — strong.

## Subsidiary theory — information (serves D7)

- **F17**: Deutsch and Marletto published "Constructor theory of information" in
  *Proceedings of the Royal Society A* (vol. 471, article 20140540), dated
  February 2015 (published online December 2014). [S7, S8] — strong.
- **F18**: In it, information is not treated as an a priori mathematical or
  logical concept; instead its nature and properties are held to be determined by
  the laws of physics alone, expressed via which transformations of physical
  systems are possible or impossible. [S7, S8] — strong.
- **F19**: The theory proposes constructor-theoretic laws capturing the
  regularities that allow information to be "physically instantiated" (carried in
  matter), aiming to underlie both classical and quantum information. [S1, S7] —
  strong.

## Subsidiary theory — life (serves D7)

- **F20**: Marletto's "Constructor Theory of Life" appeared in the *Journal of
  the Royal Society Interface* (vol. 12, article 20141226) in 2015. [S11, S12] —
  strong.
- **F21**: It addresses how neo-Darwinian evolution explains the appearance of
  design in living things *without* a designer, via replication and natural
  selection. [S11] — strong.
- **F22**: Its central result: accurate self-reproduction, replication and
  natural selection are compatible with "no-design" laws of physics (laws that
  contain no special provisions for living things), the only non-trivial
  requirement being that the laws allow **digital information** to be physically
  instantiated. [S11, S12] — strong.
- **F23**: It also argues that under no-design laws an accurate replicator
  requires a "vehicle" which, together with the replicator, constitutes a
  self-reproducer. [S11] — strong.

## Subsidiary theory — thermodynamics & probability (serves D7)

- **F24**: A constructor theory of thermodynamics has been developed (Marletto,
  arXiv 1608.02625) aiming to give an exact, scale-independent statement of the
  second law of thermodynamics in constructor-theoretic terms. [S13, S3] —
  strong (preprint + corroborated by overview).
- **F25**: Thermodynamics fits the can/can't mould naturally: some
  transformations run easily one way but not the other (the second law), and the
  framework states these as which tasks are possible vs impossible. [S6, S13] —
  medium/strong.
- **F26**: There is also a "Constructor Theory of Probability" (Marletto, arXiv
  1507.03287) extending the approach to probabilistic statements. [S14] —
  strong (preprint).

## Status, testability & criticism (serves D8)

- **F27**: Constructor theory is an early-stage research programme, not
  established or experimentally confirmed physics; Wikipedia categorizes it under
  "fringe physics," reflecting that it sits outside the mainstream. [S3] —
  medium (encyclopedic categorization, but accurately reflects status).
- **F28**: Even sympathetic coverage notes that "whether constructor theory can
  really deliver … remains to be seen," and that broad empirical validation is so
  far undeveloped. [S6] — strong.
- **F29**: A notable, more concrete spin-off: Marletto and Vedral (2017,
  *Physical Review Letters* 119, 240402) proposed that if two masses, each in a
  quantum superposition of positions, become **entangled** purely through their
  mutual gravity, that would be evidence gravity itself is quantum — because a
  purely classical mediator cannot create entanglement. [S15, S16] — strong.
- **F30**: This gravity-entanglement test is considered closer to feasible than
  detecting individual gravitons or Planck-scale effects, though it remains very
  hard and has not yet been carried out at the time of writing. [S15, S16] —
  strong (proposal + ongoing experimental literature).

## Contested / honesty notes

- The "fringe physics" label (F27) is a Wikipedia categorization, not a verdict
  from a specific authority — frame it as "outside the current mainstream and not
  yet experimentally established," which all sources support, rather than as a
  dismissal.
- F29/F30 (gravity test) is *inspired by and related to* the counterfactual /
  information line of thinking Marletto pursues, but it is not a direct test of
  "constructor theory" as a whole. Must be framed carefully: it shows the broader
  research direction makes contact with experiment, not that constructor theory
  itself has been confirmed.
