# substack.md — format: Substack / newsletter post

*Draft — `[you]` file.*

## 1. What it is / when to use
A newsletter post with a personal voice and a direct relationship to the reader
who subscribed. Roomier than an op-ed, more intimate than an essay. Use for a
take, a deep-dive, or a thinking-out-loud piece delivered to people who chose to
hear from *you*.

## 2. Required parts  (→ deliverables)
- A hook in the first lines that justifies the open (subject lines and previews
  matter).
- A clear through-line, even when the voice wanders productively.
- At least one moment of genuine voice — a stance, an admission, a turn — that a
  wire-service piece wouldn't have.
- A close that leaves the reader with something to carry, and (if apt) a light
  forward-look, never a dead "in summary."

## 3. Length norm
800–1,600 words, flexible. (Overridable.)

## 4. Visuals policy
auto — friendly to visuals. Tables, simple charts, and pull-quotes all suit the
medium. Images earn placement beside the claim they serve.

## 5. Conventions & register
First person allowed and often wanted. Subheads, short paragraphs, the
occasional aside. Conversational but not sloppy — Pinker prose with the collar
unbuttoned. Ends on a human note.

## 6. Imagery & meme licensing
Controlling metaphor as usual; the looser register tolerates more supporting
images. Memes: licensed **if the audience licenses them** — a meme that lands
for these subscribers is on-brand; one that doesn't is cringe. Evergreen over
topical. All on the imagery map.
