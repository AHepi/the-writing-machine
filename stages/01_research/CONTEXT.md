# Stage 01 — research

Gather the facts the piece will stand on, each tagged and sourced. This stage
finds and records; it does not select, argue, or write prose. Selection is
stage 02's job; the writing is stage 03's. Here, you cast wide and tag
honestly.

## READ
- `stages/00_brief/output/brief.md` — the resolved brief (topic, goal,
  audience, notes). Read it whole; it has no pointers.
- `references/research-method.md` in this folder.

## INTERNALIZE
- The brief's `goal` and `notes` tell you what facts the piece will need —
  including the facts the *strongest counterargument* needs. Research the other
  side too; an argument with no opposing facts can't steelman.
- Every fact gets an **F-tag** and names an **S-tag**. Provenance is not
  optional and not added later — it is created here, at the moment of finding.
- Do not invent, estimate, or round into precision. An unknown is recorded as
  an unknown.

## TRANSFORM
1. Identify the questions the goal and deliverables raise (including the
   opposing case).
2. Find facts that answer them (per `research-method.md`). For each: capture the
   claim, the number or quote, and exactly where it came from.
3. Assign each finding an F-tag (`F1`, `F2`…) and link it to an S-tag (`S1`,
   `S2`…) in the source list. One source can back many findings.
4. Note, per source, an S-register reliability mark (see method file): primary /
   secondary / tertiary, and any reason to treat it with caution.
5. Flag gaps: questions the deliverables need answered that you could not source.

## WRITE
- `stages/01_research/output/findings.md` — the F-tagged facts, each with its
  claim and its S-tag.
- `stages/01_research/output/sources.md` — the S-tagged sources, each with
  enough detail to find it again and its S-register mark.

## CHECK
- [ ] Every finding has an F-tag and names a real S-tag.
- [ ] Every S-tag in findings exists in `sources.md`.
- [ ] The opposing case has facts, not just the favored side.
- [ ] No invented numbers or citations; unknowns are recorded as unknowns.
- [ ] Gaps the deliverables need are listed explicitly.

## ASK HUMAN
- If a deliverable depends on a fact you cannot source at all, and the piece
  can't honestly be written without it, flag it now rather than letting stage
  03 paper over it.

## STOP
Hand `findings.md` and `sources.md` to stage 02.
