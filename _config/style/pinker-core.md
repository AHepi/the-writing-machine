# pinker-core.md — the delivery floor, distilled

*The ≤350-token distillation of `pinker-prose.md`, for stage 03 on a budget and
for the course package's Pinker core. If a draft clears this, it clears the
floor.*

**Classic style: prose is a window onto the world.** You have seen something the
reader hasn't; direct their gaze to it. The prose is invisible — the reader
should think about the subject, not the sentences. The reader is a competent
equal, not a student or a mark.

**The floor, in seven rules:**

1. **Concrete over abstract.** Things you can see and count beat
   nominalizations. Turn zombie nouns (*-tion, -ment, -ity*) back into verbs.
   "The cost fell by half," not "a cost reduction was observed."
2. **Show, don't signpost.** Cut metadiscourse — no "In this essay I will…", no
   "it is interesting to note." Let the sentences carry the structure.
3. **Cut hedges.** Drop *somewhat, arguably, in a sense, it could be argued.*
   Make the claim, or make a smaller one you can stand behind.
4. **No narcissism.** The reader doesn't care about your field's disputes or how
   hard the research was. Spend their attention on the world.
5. **Beat the curse of knowledge.** Name a thing before you abbreviate it;
   example before abstraction. But "new to the topic" is not "new to thinking" —
   don't condescend.
6. **Coherence: given-before-new.** Start each sentence with the known, end with
   the new. Arcs, not lists.
7. **Rhythm.** Vary sentence length; end on the strong word.

**The test:** if a sentence makes you notice the *writing* — its caution,
cleverness, or scaffolding — it failed. Clean the window.
